Spin the Wheel

Originally started as a joke at the request of a roommate, this project continues to develop. Spin the Wheel is an excuse to learn Python and make use of some Raspberry Pis. The goal of the project is to detect the opening and closing of doors in my home and trigger smart home events based on these. Some of the functions are useful, such as making sure Hue lights are not left on or appliances that are running when they shouldn't be. GMRS Repeater

The objective of this project was to create an analog "poor man's repeater" that could be moved and easily deployed by just about anyone. The final result was a durable and weather-resistant 50-watt package that can be activated at the flick of a switch and a 120-volt power supply. A future revision aims to add 12-volt support for running the radios off of a low voltage supply such as a car, as well as the ability to self identify on its chosen frequencies by means of a Raspberry Pi and custom software. Although this project was only loosely related to computing, I had a wonderful time creating it.
